HHS OCR Imposes $2.1M Penalty Against Virginia Health System for EHR Access Control Failures

The HHS Office for Civil Rights announced a $2.1 million civil monetary penalty against a Virginia-based regional health system for systemic failures in EHR access control management spanning 2022–2024. OCR investigators found the covered entity had failed to implement role-based access controls, resulting in unauthorized workforce member access to over 14,000 patient records. The settlement includes a 2-year corrective action plan with mandatory quarterly compliance reporting to OCR. Security experts note the access control gap mirrors patterns identified in a 2024 OCR guidance bulletin, suggesting this enforcement action may signal an increased scrutiny cycle.

Senate SAVE Act Advances Out of HELP Committee; Floor Vote Expected This Summer

The Workplace Violence Prevention for Health Care and Social Service Workers Act (SAVE Act) advanced out of the Senate HELP Committee on a bipartisan 14-9 vote Friday, setting up a potential floor vote before the August recess. The bill would require covered healthcare employers to develop and implement comprehensive workplace violence prevention plans under OSHA oversight, with civil penalties for non-compliance. The American Hospital Association has expressed qualified support while seeking amendments on small and rural hospital compliance timelines. Nursing unions are pushing for the bill to pass without weakening amendments.
