Update ASIS International (Security Management) June 1, 2026

Make security video and analytics defensible: governance, access controls, and audit trails

ASIS highlights a growing governance gap: ad hoc video exports, broad access, and indefinite retention create liability and privacy risks. For hospitals and universities expanding cameras, body-worn video, and analytics, the piece outlines practices that make systems ‘defensible’: formal request/approval workflows, role-based access, immutable audit logs for every export/view, calibrated retention tied to purpose, and periodic reviews. Security leaders should treat video and analytics as high-risk data platforms, not just devices—establish data owners, classify feeds (e.g., clinical areas vs. public spaces), and integrate with incident reporting. This reduces discovery exposure, supports Title IX and workplace violence investigations, and aligns with evolving AI and privacy standards.
