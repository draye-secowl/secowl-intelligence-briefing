Critical KQED June 8, 2026

Cal/OSHA fines San Francisco General and UCSF over fatal clinic stabbing, cites WPV plan gaps

California OSHA issued $130,500 in fines to Zuckerberg San Francisco General Hospital and $142,700 to UCSF following the December 2025 fatal stabbing of a social worker at Ward 86. Inspectors cited missing or insufficient workplace violence prevention measures, including failure to develop a safety plan after threats, inadequate staff notifications, and lack of cameras or weapons screening. The report notes delayed reporting to Cal/OSHA and deficiencies in violent-incident logs. City officials say they’ve added security staff, created a threat management team, and installed metal detectors; the city also committed new funding for safety upgrades. Why it matters: compliance, security, and risk leaders should review WPV programs against Cal/OSHA and Joint Commission requirements, validate incident logging/notification workflows, and close ED/clinic access-control gaps quickly.
